A Brief Description of Each Character

  • A small, intricately carved wooden bird: A family heirloom given to Elara. It represents hope, freedom, and the enduring spirit of their heritage.

  • A small, simple ring forged from scrap metal: Secretly made by David. It symbolizes his unwavering loyalty, his vow to find his friends, and the unbreakable bond of their friendship.

  • An embroidered handkerchief: Sarah's mother's gift, hidden by Sarah. It represents comfort, a mother's love, and the personal, intimate memories of a home that was lost.

  • A single page from "The Little Prince" with a drawing of a rose: Ripped out by Samuel. It symbolizes the preservation of innocence, beauty, and the memory of a world that once was.
